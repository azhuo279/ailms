# AI Experience Designer Design Task

**Organization:** BCG X | Experience Design  
**Season:** Spring 2026  
**Selected Prompt:** Option 1 - The Operations Co-Pilot

## Purpose

This brief is for you to demonstrate your credentials as an AI Experience Designer.

The selected prompt is an AI product design challenge. You will have 72 hours to complete the take-home, then present in a 60-minute live session with members of the BCG X team.

You are encouraged to use AI tools throughout your process, such as Claude, Figma Make, Cursor, v0, or whatever fits. Be ready to talk about how you used them.

## Option 1: The Operations Co-Pilot

### Scenario

A global logistics company is deploying a model to help operations managers handle exceptions, including delayed shipments, customs holds, capacity crunches, and related operational issues, across 400+ warehouses.

Today, operations managers spend 60% of the day triaging alerts across 6 disconnected tools. They miss things, act late, and burn out. The company has asked BCG X to design an AI co-pilot that helps operations managers prioritize and act on what matters most.

### Ask

Design the core experience of the AI co-pilot. Your work should show:

- How the AI surfaces and prioritizes exceptions, not just lists them
- How the manager understands why the AI flagged something
- How the manager acts, overrides, or delegates, and what the AI does next
- What happens when the AI gets a prioritization wrong

### What We're Testing

#### AI Thinking

Show us how you reason about how the solution behaves, what it looks like, and how it solves the core problem.

#### Trust and Transparency

Design for the moments when AI might be wrong. How do users know, and what can they do?

#### Strategic Framing

Show us how you frame the business problem, define the user opportunity, and make your prioritization decision.

#### Craft

We care deeply about the quality of your work: clarity, visual polish, and usability.

#### Your Process

Use AI tools in your work, such as Claude, Figma Make, Cursor, v0, or whatever fits. The team will ask about it in the debrief.

### Format and Timing

- **Take-home:** 72 hours
- **Live session:** 60 minutes
- **Live-session focus:** Lo-fi flows and rationale, followed by presentation and iteration with the interviewer

## Brief

Conceive a product opportunity related to AI and digital that focuses on **Option 1: The Operations Co-Pilot**, with emphasis on product and experience design.

## What Goes Into a Strong Submission

### 1. User and Context

Define the user, their needs, behaviors, pain points, and the business context and success metric.

### 2. A Strong Case

Explain why a model is the right approach here. What does it unlock over rules or a person? Where should it not be used?

### 3. Flows and Hi-Fi

Include core journeys, wireframes, and at least three hi-fi screens. The work should be consistent, accessible, and polished.

### 4. Your Process

Show sketches, notes, prompts, and iterations. Explain how modern tools shaped the work, not just the polished output.

> **Note:** Step 4, the AI design process, is the key differentiator for BCG X. Strong candidates will show their AI workflow explicitly, not just the output. Use of Claude, Figma Make, Cursor, Replit, or equivalent tools during the exercise is encouraged and will be discussed in the debrief.

### 5. Next Steps

Given a few more weeks with a small team, describe what the team would validate, test, or build next.

## Presentation Reminder

You will have 35 minutes to present your product idea/solution, followed by a 25-minute discussion with the team.

## How to Approach the 72 Hours

### 01. Understand the Prompt

Read Option 1 carefully. Define the user, the AI's role, and the core tension before designing anything.

### 02. Use AI Tools

Use Claude, Figma Make, Cursor, v0, or whichever tools help you think and build faster. You will be asked about your process in the live session.

### 03. Document Your Approach

Document your process as you go, including sketches, prompts, and iterations. This is as important as the final output.

### 04. Prioritize Quality Over Quantity

Three strong, polished hi-fi screens beat ten rough ones. BCG X is evaluating judgment, not volume.
